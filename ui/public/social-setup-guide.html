Social Platform Setup Guide

How to create developer apps on Twitter/X, LinkedIn, and Meta to enable API-based posting. Facebook and Instagram work automatically via browser — this guide is for API connection (optional, advanced).

𝕏 Twitter / X
Heads up: X requires a paid developer plan to use the API. You'll need to subscribe at developer.twitter.com/en/portal/products before your app can post.

Step 1 — Create a developer account

  1. 1

    Go to developer.twitter.com/en/portal/dashboard and sign in with your X account.

  2. 2

    If this is your first time, you'll be asked to set an Account Name (this is just a label for your developer account, not your @handle) and fill in the "Describe all of your use cases" field. It requires at least 100 characters — something like: "Personal AI assistant that reads my timeline and posts scheduled content on my behalf. Used only for my own account. No data resale or third-party access."

  3. 3

    Check all three agreement boxes and click Submit.

Step 2 — Create an app

  1. 1

    From the dashboard, click Create App (or + Add App if you're inside a Project).

  2. 2

    Give it a name like My Adjutant.

  3. 3

    When asked to choose an environment, select Development — this is fine for personal use and doesn't require any review process.

  4. 4

    After the app is created, X will show you a Bearer Token, Consumer Key, and Consumer Secret. These are for OAuth 1.0a — you don't need them. You can dismiss or save them somewhere safe, but Adjutant doesn't use them.

Step 3 — Set up User Authentication (OAuth 2.0)

  1. 1

    From your app's panel in the dashboard, scroll down to User authentication settings and click Set up.

  2. 2

    Under App permissions, select Read, Write, and Direct Message.

  3. 3

    Under Type of App, select Web App, Automated App or Bot.

  4. 4

    Set Callback URI / Redirect URL to: http://localhost:8001/api/oauth/callback

  5. 5

    Set Website URL to anything valid (e.g. https://example.com).

  6. 6

    Click Save. X will now show you an OAuth 2.0 Client ID and Client Secret — copy both.

What to paste into Adjutant

Client ID→paste into the Client ID field
Client Secret→paste into the Client Secret field
Note: The Consumer Key / Bearer Token shown right after app creation are OAuth 1.0a credentials — ignore those. The Client ID and Client Secret only appear after you complete the User authentication settings step above.
🔵 LinkedIn

Step 1 — Create a LinkedIn app

  1. 1

    Go to linkedin.com/developers/apps/new and sign in.

  2. 2

    Fill in the App name (e.g. My Adjutant). You need to associate it with a LinkedIn Page — a personal page works fine. Upload any logo, accept the terms, then click Create app.

  3. 3

    Once the app is created, click the Auth tab.

Step 2 — Set the redirect URL

  1. 1

    Under OAuth 2.0 settings → Authorized redirect URLs, click the pencil icon and add: http://localhost:8001/api/oauth/callback

  2. 2

    Click Update.

  3. 3

    Your Client ID and Client Secret are shown at the top of the Auth tab.

Step 3 — Request required products

  1. 1

    Click the Products tab.

  2. 2

    Find Sign In with LinkedIn using OpenID Connect and click Request access. This grants the openid, profile, and email scopes needed to identify your account.

  3. 3

    Also find Share on LinkedIn and click Request access. This grants w_member_social for posting. Both are usually approved instantly.

What to paste into Adjutant

Client ID→paste into the Client ID field
Client Secret→paste into the Client Secret field
Note: The Client Secret is only shown once when the app is first created. If you missed it, go to the Auth tab → Application credentials and click Regenerate.
🌐 Meta (Facebook & Instagram)
You don't need to do any of this to get started. Adjutant posts to Facebook and Instagram automatically using browser automation — no developer account, no app review, no waiting. This guide is only for users who want to connect via Meta's API for faster background posting without a browser window opening.
This process takes time. Meta requires three prerequisites before API access is granted: Business Verification, App Review, and Advanced Access approval. Plan for 1–5 business days per step. Complete them in order.

Phase 1 — Create your Facebook Page

  1. 1

    Do not create a standalone Facebook account for Adjutant — Meta rejects these. Instead, sign in to your personal Facebook account.

  2. 2

    Go to facebook.com/pages/create and create a Page for your brand or project (e.g. My Adjutant). This is what Adjutant will post to — not your personal profile.

  3. 3

    If you want Instagram posting, make sure your Instagram account is a Professional (Business or Creator) account and connect it to this Page: Page settings → Linked accounts → Instagram.

Phase 2 — Create a Meta developer app

  1. 1

    Go to developers.facebook.com/apps/create and sign in with your personal Facebook account.

  2. 2

    When asked for a use case, select Other, then click Next.

  3. 3

    Select Business as the app type, then click Next.

  4. 4

    Enter an app name (e.g. My Adjutant), leave Business Account blank for now, and click Create app.

Phase 3 — Find your App ID and App Secret

  1. 1

    In the left sidebar, go to App settings → Basic.

  2. 2

    Your App ID is shown at the top. Copy it.

  3. 3

    Click Show next to App secret, verify your password, and copy it.

  4. 4

    Fill in the required fields Meta needs before you can submit for review: Privacy Policy URL (a simple hosted page is fine), App Icon (1024×1024px), and Category. Click Save changes.

Phase 4 — Add Facebook Login for Business & set redirect URI

  1. 1

    In the left sidebar, click Add product, find Facebook Login for Business, and click Set up.

  2. 2

    Choose Web, enter http://localhost:8001 as the site URL, and click Save.

  3. 3

    In the Facebook Login for Business settings, under Valid OAuth Redirect URIs, add: http://localhost:8001/api/oauth/callback — then click Save changes.

Phase 5 — Business Verification (prerequisite for App Review)

  1. 1

    Go to business.facebook.com/settings and create a Meta Business Account if you don't already have one.

  2. 2

    In Business settings, go to Security Center and click Start Verification under Business Verification.

  3. 3

    You'll need to provide: your legal business name, address, phone number, and either a business document (utility bill, bank statement, business license) or domain verification via DNS record or meta tag.

  4. 4

    Once submitted, Meta typically reviews within 1–5 business days. You'll receive an email when approved.

  5. 5

    After approval, return to your app in the developer portal → App settings → Basic and associate it with your verified Business Account.

Phase 6 — App Review (request Advanced Access)

  1. 1

    In the developer portal left sidebar, go to App Review → Permissions and Features.

  2. 2

    Find and request Advanced Access for each of the following permissions. Click Request Advanced Access next to each one:

pages_show_list

Discover which Pages you manage. Required for the Connect flow to find your Page.

pages_manage_posts

Create and publish posts to your Facebook Page.

pages_read_engagement

Read basic Page data needed during the connection process.

instagram_basic

Discover the Instagram Business account linked to your Page.

instagram_content_publish

Publish photo and text posts to Instagram.

  1. 3

    For each permission, Meta will ask you to provide a screencast demonstrating the permission being used. Record yourself: opening Adjutant → going to Settings → Connections → clicking Connect next to Facebook/Instagram → completing the OAuth flow → having Adjutant post to your Page. A simple screen recording with narration is sufficient.

  2. 4

    Submit for review. Meta typically responds within 1–5 business days.

Phase 7 — Advanced Access verification

  1. 1

    After App Review approval, Meta may require a separate Advanced Access verification step under App Review → Advanced Access. This confirms your use case aligns with Meta's policies.

  2. 2

    Describe your use case honestly: "Personal AI assistant that posts content to my own Facebook Page and Instagram Business account on my behalf. No third-party users. No data resale."

  3. 3

    Once all permissions show Advanced Access: Approved, your app is fully set up. Switch the app from Development to Live mode in App settings → Basic.

What to paste into Adjutant

App ID→paste into the App ID field
App Secret→paste into the App Secret field
While waiting for review: You can still use Adjutant to post to Facebook/Instagram via browser automation — no API credentials needed. Once Advanced Access is approved and you complete the Connect flow, Adjutant will automatically switch to the API for faster, more reliable posting.
